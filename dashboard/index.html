Thesis

Premium automotive brands in Portugal are generating measurable, in some cases exceptional, digital demand вЂ” but losing buyers at the same structural funnel points. Their digital infrastructure varies significantly in readiness for the direct-to-consumer transition that is already underway across the segment. This gap between demand capture and conversion readiness is the central finding.

No brand in this comparison has activated online purchase in Portugal as of 27/06/2026. The thesis holds uniformly across all five brands.

GA4 proxy baseline

Source: Google BigQuery public dataset вЂ” Google Merchandise Store GA4 export. Sessions: 344,640. This dataset establishes three structural patterns common to high-consideration digital purchase funnels: drop-off concentration between product view and first commitment action; a 51.5Г— return visitor conversion lift; and a channel-specific mobile gap. These are structural patterns only вЂ” not automotive data, not Portuguese market data, not any brand's actual performance.

No brand-specific conclusion is stated as if it derives from this dataset. The proxy establishes the pattern. Direct observation and search data establish the brand-specific finding.

Google Trends Portugal

Relative search interest indexed to 100. All figures include the exact observation date and query string. Search demand data is used as the primary proxy for buyer intent in the Portuguese market вЂ” it is direct, market-specific evidence rather than survey inference. Trends data is relative and time-bound — figures are valid at the observation date only and are not presented as durable statistics.

Index instability caveat: Google Trends indices are relative, not absolute. Each value represents search interest relative to the peak within the selected time window for that specific query. The same query observed on a different date or with a different time window will return a different index value. Indices across different queries and brands are directional indicators of demand tier, not precise comparable measurements. BREAKOUT status and percentage figures in rising queries are signals of acceleration, not exact counts. All index values in this audit are stated with their observation date; they are used to establish relative demand tiers only and no claim depends on any figure being stable or replicable on re-observation.

Direct configurator observation

Each configurator walked on desktop (Chrome, 1440px) and mobile (Chrome, 390px) on the same date, using a ten-checkpoint fieldwork checklist. Screenshots taken at each decision point. This is primary evidence and carries more weight than proxy inferences.

BMW Group Report 2024, Audi Group Combined Annual and Sustainability Report 2024, Mercedes-Benz Group Annual Report 2024, and Volvo Cars Annual Report 2024 were accessed and extracted. Three Testable Hypotheses

H1

Configurator end CTA is the single highest-value optimisation point

All five brands show measurable to exceptional search demand, confirmed configurator sessions, and no live digital purchase option in Portugal as of 27/06/2026. The end CTA is the common structural lever across all five.

The iX3 vs i4 finding sharpens H1: BMW has demonstrated it can deploy a digital commitment CTA (i4 "Reservar") within the same brand infrastructure. The iX3 вЂ” the higher-demand model вЂ” lacks it. This is not a capability gap. It is a deployment decision. The implication is that the CTA infrastructure exists; the barrier is rollout prioritisation, not technical readiness.

Named metric: Lead submission rate at 7, 14, and 30 days after configurator completion. Limitation: requires brand analytics access вЂ” not available in this audit. Flagged as the primary question that real data access would answer.
H2

Save mechanism friction suppresses return visit value

The 51.5Г— return visitor conversion lift in the GA4 proxy establishes that high-intent returners are the most valuable cohort in high-consideration digital purchase funnels. The Audi carLOG finding is the clearest evidence of friction at this point: a third-party save portal with an explicit configuration expiry warning is the only save mechanism in this comparison with no confirmed frictionless alternative вЂ” and the one most likely to fail a buyer who returns to their saved configuration after a product update.

The friction ranking (Volvo lowest в†’ Mercedes highest) maps inversely to the presence and prominence of frictionless secondary paths. All five brands have identified this problem to some degree вЂ” all offer at least one frictionless alternative. None has eliminated the account requirement for primary save.

Named metric: Return visit rate for configurator sessions segmented by save mechanism type. Limitation: requires brand analytics access вЂ” not available in this audit.
H3 Invalid

Volvo PT is the most digitally advanced funnel and the thesis exception

H3 is invalid. Volvo PT end CTA is "Pedir proposta" вЂ” dealer form only. The /shop/ URL architecture is built and present on individual stock vehicle pages; the purchase action available is "Pedir Proposta", not a checkout flow. Online purchase is not live in Portugal as of 27/06/2026.

The thesis holds uniformly: no brand in this comparison has activated online purchase in Portugal. Volvo's /shop/ URL is the closest signal that e-commerce infrastructure has been built вЂ” but it has not been activated. H3 is not simply false; the finding is that Volvo is further along the infrastructure path than any other brand, while still resolving to a dealer form at the point of commitment.

Evidence: Direct observation 27/06/2026. Volvo Cars Annual Report 2024: strategy explicitly moved away from full market conversion after UK pilot вЂ” "a more pragmatic approach in collaboration with our strong retailer network." Portugal not named as a converted or planned market.
