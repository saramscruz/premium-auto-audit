Thesis

Premium automotive brands in Portugal are generating measurable, in some cases exceptional, digital demand â€” but losing buyers at the same structural funnel points. Their digital infrastructure varies significantly in readiness for the direct-to-consumer transition that is already underway across the segment. This gap between demand capture and conversion readiness is the central finding.

The counter-argument deserves to be named explicitly: the annual reports of all five brands suggest that Portugal's non-activation may be deliberate positioning rather than a missed opportunity. Mercedes-Benz had converted eleven European markets to direct sales by end 2024 but has not named Portugal. Volvo explicitly abandoned full-market conversion after its UK pilot, choosing instead to digitise within the existing retailer network. Porsche's "Value over Volume" strategy and Destination Porsche retail concept confirm the dealer model as permanent at this price point. BMW states directly that BMW brand direct sales in Europe are "planned for a later date." Audi calls its dealer network "the backbone of our sales and distribution." The thesis does not require these brands to have made a mistake. It requires only that the structural gap between demand and conversion readiness is real and measurable — which the configurator observation confirms regardless of whether the gap is intentional.

No brand in this comparison has activated online purchase in Portugal as of 27/06/2026. The thesis holds uniformly across all five brands.

Three Testable Hypotheses

H1

Configurator end CTA is the single highest-value optimisation point

All five brands show measurable to exceptional search demand, confirmed configurator sessions, and no live digital purchase option in Portugal as of 27/06/2026. The end CTA is the common structural lever across all five.

The iX3 vs i4 finding sharpens H1: BMW has demonstrated it can deploy a digital commitment CTA (i4 "Reservar") within the same brand infrastructure. The iX3 â€” the higher-demand model â€” lacks it. This is not a capability gap. It is a deployment decision — the most parsimonious reading given that the infrastructure demonstrably exists within the same market and brand. The implication is that the barrier is rollout prioritisation, not technical readiness. Alternative explanations (iX3 product lifecycle phase, inventory model dependency, market-sequenced rollout) cannot be ruled out without internal confirmation.

Named metric: Lead submission rate at 7, 14, and 30 days after configurator completion. Limitation: requires brand analytics access â€” not available in this audit. Flagged as the primary question that real data access would answer.
H2

Save mechanism friction suppresses return visit value

The GA4 proxy shows a 6.3x conversion lift from first to sixth-plus session (0.78% to 4.94%) in the Google Merchandise Store dataset. This establishes that return visit depth — not simply first vs return — is the relevant variable: conversion compounds progressively across sessions without plateauing through session six. The Audi carLOG finding is the clearest evidence of friction at this point: a third-party save portal with an explicit configuration expiry warning is the only save mechanism in this comparison with no confirmed frictionless alternative â€” and the one most likely to fail a buyer who returns to their saved configuration after a product update.

The friction ranking (Volvo lowest â†’ Mercedes highest) maps inversely to the presence and prominence of frictionless secondary paths. All five brands have identified this problem to some degree â€” all offer at least one frictionless alternative. None has eliminated the account requirement for primary save.

Named metric: Return visit rate for configurator sessions segmented by save mechanism type. Limitation: requires brand analytics access â€” not available in this audit.
H3 Invalid

Volvo PT is the most digitally advanced funnel and the thesis exception

H3 is invalid. Volvo PT end CTA is "Pedir proposta" â€” dealer form only. The /shop/ URL architecture is built and present on individual stock vehicle pages; the purchase action available is "Pedir Proposta", not a checkout flow. Online purchase is not live in Portugal as of 27/06/2026.

The thesis holds uniformly: no brand in this comparison has activated online purchase in Portugal. Volvo's /shop/ URL is the closest signal that e-commerce infrastructure has been built â€” but it has not been activated. H3 is not simply false; the finding is that Volvo is further along the infrastructure path than any other brand, while still resolving to a dealer form at the point of commitment.

Evidence: Direct observation 27/06/2026. Volvo Cars Annual Report 2024: strategy explicitly moved away from full market conversion after UK pilot â€” "a more pragmatic approach in collaboration with our strong retailer network." Portugal not named as a converted or planned market.
