Thesis

Premium automotive brands in Portugal are generating measurable, in some cases exceptional, digital demand — but losing buyers at the same structural funnel points. Their digital infrastructure varies significantly in readiness for the direct-to-consumer transition that is already underway across the segment. This gap between demand capture and conversion readiness is the central finding.

Methodology

GA4 proxy baseline

Source: Google BigQuery public dataset — Google Merchandise Store GA4 export. Sessions: 344,640. This dataset establishes three structural patterns common to high-consideration digital purchase funnels: drop-off concentration between product view and first commitment action; a 51.5× return visitor conversion lift; and a channel-specific mobile gap. These are structural patterns only — not automotive data, not Portuguese market data, not any brand's actual performance.

No brand-specific conclusion is stated as if it derives from this dataset. The proxy establishes the pattern. Direct observation and search data establish the brand-specific finding.

Google Trends Portugal

Relative search interest indexed to 100. All figures include the exact observation date and query string. Trends data is relative and time-bound — figures are valid at the observation date only and are not presented as durable statistics.

Direct configurator observation

Each configurator walked on desktop (Chrome, 1440px) and mobile (Chrome, 390px) on the same date, using the Section 6 checklist. Screenshots taken at each decision point. This is primary evidence and carries more weight than proxy inferences.

Annual reports 2024

BMW Group Report 2024, Audi Group Combined Annual and Sustainability Report 2024, and equivalent reports for Mercedes-Benz, Volvo Cars, and Porsche AG. Used for direct sales status and stated digital commerce strategy. Annual reports describe stated strategy — configurator observation determines what is live in Portugal.

Cox Automotive 2024 Car Buyer Journey Study

n=2,310, published January 2025. US market study. Used for directional context on research duration (13.5+ hours) and websites visited (4.1 average). Directional applicability to Portugal is argued, not assumed.

Three Testable Hypotheses

H1 — Configurator end CTA is the single highest-value optimisation point

If all five brands show high search demand, completed configurator sessions, and no digital commitment option — the end CTA is the common lever. If one or more brands have solved it, the hypothesis becomes: brands with a digital commitment CTA show measurably higher re-engagement or lead quality.

Evidence required: End CTA observation for all five brands (matrix row 1).

Metric: Lead submission rate at 14 days — requires brand analytics access. Flagged as limitation.

Status: Cannot be stated until matrix row 1 is complete.

H2 — Save mechanism friction suppresses return visit value

The 51.5× return visitor lift (proxy, structural) means high-intent returners are the most valuable cohort. The Audi carLOG finding suggests the save mechanism — not the retargeting window — is the primary barrier.

Evidence required: Save mechanism classification for all five brands (matrix row 2).

Metric: Return visit rate for configurator sessions by save mechanism type — requires brand analytics. Flagged as limitation.

Status: Cannot be stated until matrix row 2 is complete.

H3 — Volvo PT is the most digitally advanced funnel and the thesis exception

If Volvo has live online purchase in Portugal, it is the only brand in the comparison where the full funnel exists digitally — the control case against which the others are measured. Including it as an exception strengthens the thesis rather than undermining it.

Evidence required: Volvo PT configurator end screen observation + Volvo Cars 2024 Annual Report.

Decision: If Volvo PT does not have online purchase live, H3 is invalid and the thesis is uniformly supported.

Status: Cannot be stated until Volvo fieldwork is complete.

What Changes With Real Data Access

This audit is built on public signals and direct observation. The following questions cannot be answered without access to brand-owned analytics:

  • Actual configurator session completion rate per brand
  • Lead submission rate at 7, 14, and 30 days after configurator completion
  • Return visit rate segmented by save mechanism type
  • CPC mobile vs desktop conversion rate by landing page type
  • Session-to-lead latency distribution (proxy shows 129h average in e-commerce; automotive equivalent unknown)
