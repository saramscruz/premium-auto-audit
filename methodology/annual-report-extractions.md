## BMW Group Report 2024 — extracted statements

**Source:** BMW Group Report 2024, Combined Management Report

### Direct sales status
- MINI brand: direct sales live in 10 European markets as of end 2024 (Italy, Poland, Sweden, Finland, Norway, France, Austria, Belgium, Luxembourg, Germany). Five additional markets went live in early March 2025.
- BMW brand: "The introduction of direct sales for the BMW brand is planned for a later date in Europe."
- Portugal: not named in MINI rollout list. Status: not confirmed live in PT as of report date.

### Digital commerce strategy
- "Customers will soon be able to choose whether to initiate the ordering process for their vehicle with a sales partner or online."
- "The BMW Group is decisively and consistently advancing the digital sale (online sale) of vehicles."
- "Customer Channels, Digital Commerce, Connected Company" unit established within sales strategy department.
- BMW Portugal Lda. and BMW Renting (Portugal) Lda. listed as 100% subsidiaries — confirms direct presence in PT.

### Implication for document
The annual report confirms BMW brand direct sales in Europe is not yet live. Portugal is not in the MINI rollout. The i4 "Reservar" CTA observed in the configurator is a reservation function within the existing dealer model, not evidence of direct sales. The report and the observation are consistent.

## Audi Group Report 2024 — extracted statements

**Source:** Audi Group Combined Annual and Sustainability Report 2024

### Direct sales status
No direct sales launch announced. The report explicitly positions the dealer network as the primary sales channel:
- "Our global dealerships play a key role in this forward-looking approach. They are the backbone of our sales and distribution."
- "Our dealerships are the key interface to our customers, whether in relation to sales and consultancy services or after sales services."
- "The dealership is a crucial asset in the context of such change."

### Digital strategy
- Digitalization mentioned in context of vehicle features and internal processes, not direct-to-consumer sales.
- "Audi on demand rent" and "Audi on demand subscribe" — mobility-as-a-service offerings, not vehicle purchase.
- No online ordering or direct purchase language found.

### Implication for document
The Audi annual report makes no claim to direct sales or online purchase capability. The dealer-form-only end CTA observed in the configurator is fully consistent with stated strategy. Audi is the most explicit of the five brands in affirming the dealer model as primary and future.

## Mercedes-Benz Group Annual Report 2024 — extracted statements
**Source:** Mercedes-Benz Group Annual Report 2024, Combined Management Report

### Direct sales status
- Programme titled "Sales of the Future" — switching countries to direct sales model.
- Germany and United Kingdom converted in 2023.
- As of 31/12/2024: eleven passenger car markets converted to direct sales total.
- Approximately 55% of European passenger car sales converted (unchanged from prior year).
- Portugal not named in any converted market list.
- Mercedes-Benz Portugal, S.A. (Sintra) listed as 100% subsidiary — direct presence confirmed.
- Mercedes-Benz.io Portugal Unipessoal Lda. (Lisbon) listed as 100% subsidiary.

### Online Store
- Online Store platform confirmed as existing infrastructure.
- Mercedes-Benz Vans explicitly uses the Cars Online Store platform for van sales.
- Portugal configurator observation: "Ir para loja online" leads to mercedes-benz.pt/passengercars/buy/
  but sole CTA is "Solicitar proposta" — dealer form. Purchase not activated in PT as of 27/06/2026.

### Implication for document
Eleven-market rollout confirms the infrastructure and will exist. Portugal's absence from the
named list is not an oversight — it is a sequencing decision. The thesis counter-argument
is grounded here: non-activation in PT may be deliberate positioning, not a gap.

## Volvo Cars Annual Report 2024 — extracted statements
**Source:** Volvo Cars Annual and Sustainability Report 2024

### Direct sales strategy — post-UK pivot
- UK market direct-to-consumer transition described as "a highly valuable project."
- Explicit strategic reversal: "rather than converting complete markets, we will work on
  digitising our business."
- Described as "a more pragmatic approach in collaboration with our strong retailer network
  and partner ecosystem."
- Volvo Car Portugal S.A. listed as subsidiary — direct presence confirmed.
- Portugal not named as a converted or planned direct sales market.

### Digital strategy
- volvocars.com described as "the foundation" of the omni-channel model.
- Retail network described as "fully engaged and optimised" — not being replaced.
- Unified digital architecture and data framework described as strategic priority.
- "Omni-channel" language used throughout — digital and physical treated as complementary.

### Implication for document
The post-UK pivot is the clearest evidence in this comparison that full market conversion
is not the universal direction. Volvo explicitly chose collaboration with the retailer network
over direct conversion. The /shop/ URL observed in PT is infrastructure built during or
after the UK pilot — present but not activated. H3 invalid finding is grounded here.

## Porsche AG Annual and Sustainability Report 2024 — extracted statements
**Source:** Porsche AG Annual and Sustainability Report 2024 (Consolidated, IFRS)
**Note:** This is the full Annual and Sustainability Report — not the standalone financial
statements. Strategy narrative is present and extractable.

### Direct sales status
- "Indirect online sales for the Porsche AG Group are conducted via its digital platform
  and sales partners." — no direct-to-consumer purchase model.
- Digital vehicle search accessible in over 100 markets — search only, not purchase.
- Porsche dealers use the platform to offer immediately available new and used vehicles online.
- Sales network: over 900 sales partners in more than 120 markets.

### Destination Porsche retail concept
- Rolled out in more than 150 of over 800 Porsche centers worldwide since end of 2020.
- Approximately 500 further centers to follow by end of the decade.
- All brick-and-mortar retail formats follow this concept — dealer model is permanent.

### Value over Volume
- CEO Oliver Blume: "Growth at any cost is not how we do things."
- "We're much more interested in following the principle of 'Value over Volume.'"
- "Always produce one vehicle fewer than the market needs."
- Confirmed in context of China decline: "We remain true to our objective, 'Value over Volume.'"

### Implication for document
Porsche is the clearest case where the thesis does not apply in the same way.
At 108,016EUR+, the absence of a direct purchase CTA is confirmed strategy, not
infrastructure lag. The Destination Porsche retail rollout (500 more centres planned)
confirms the dealer model is the primary format through the end of the decade.
